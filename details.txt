Project: LoanLens
Repository: https://github.com/KausaniPyne/loanlens

Description:
LoanLens is an enterprise-grade, high-performance benchmarking platform engineered to bring radical, data-driven transparency to the home loan market. It shifts the fintech paradigm away from simple loan origination and toward post-disbursement auditing. 

By analyzing a dataset of over 100,000 synthetic home loan profiles, the system evaluates active loans to definitively ascertain if a borrower's interest rate is market-fair or an overpayment. Instead of relying on generalized market averages, LoanLens runs an unsupervised K-Means clustering algorithm to match a borrower to their exact financial cohort (by segmenting CIBIL score, loan-to-value ratio, city tier, and employment type). Once the cohort is verified, a high-speed Dual-Layer Stacked Ensemble (CatBoost + TabNet Neural Networks) calculates an absolute "Fair Rate".

If a borrower is actively overpaying (the "RED" verdict), the system dynamically calculates their lifetime financial loss and autonomously generates an actionable negotiation script, detailed balance-transfer breakeven paths, and a strategic CIBIL roadmap.

Under the hood, LoanLens operates on a state-of-the-art, fully containerized infrastructure built with a Next.js 14 frontend and a FastAPI (Python 3.11) backend. The strict microservice architecture utilizes PostgreSQL 15 for data persistence, Redis 7 for high-speed corridor caching, and MinIO for S3-compatible model object storage, natively supporting sub-15ms machine learning inference latencies.
